Example program for zunmtr

This example computes the two smallest eigenvalues, and the associated eigenvectors, of the matrix A, where
A = -2.28+0.00i 1.78-2.03i 2.26+0.10i -0.12+2.53i 1.78+2.03i -1.12+0.00i 0.01+0.43i -1.07+0.86i 2.26-0.10i 0.01-0.43i -0.37+0.00i 2.31-0.92i -0.12-2.53i -1.07-0.86i 2.31+0.92i -0.73+0.00i .  
Here A is Hermitian and must first be reduced to tridiagonal form T by zhetrd. The program then calls dstebz to compute the requested eigenvalues and zstein to compute the associated eigenvectors of T. Finally zunmtr is called to transform the eigenvectors to those of A.
